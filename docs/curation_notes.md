# Curation Notes

## Goal of the Curated Folder

Turn a course capstone workspace into a portfolio-friendly case study that highlights analytical judgment, reproducibility, and communication.

## What Was Kept

- The consolidated visualization notebook because it already groups multiple charts and short interpretations.
- Custom Python scripts that generate dashboard assets and presentation figures.
- Dashboard exports and selected figure outputs.
- The original data-cleaning strategy as supporting methodology.
- The cleaned dashboard-ready CSV outputs derived from the survey dataset.

## What Was Intentionally Left Out

- Course labs with vendor branding and assignment instructions.
- Intermediate files whose main purpose is classroom progression rather than portfolio storytelling.
- Large numbers of near-duplicate notebooks that dilute the main narrative.

## Initial Weaknesses Still Present

- The notebook still contains traces of lab framing and should be rewritten later.
- The project needs a stronger business-facing problem statement and audience definition.
- Some findings are descriptive but not yet turned into recommendations or decision support.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. Rewrite the notebook introduction and section titles with a professional narrative.
2. Create a short `executive_summary.md` or final notebook that states the business question, method, findings, and limitations.
3. Replace generic chart titles with portfolio-quality titles and captions.
4. Decide whether to publish this as a standalone repository or as one project folder inside a broader portfolio repository.
